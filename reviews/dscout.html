What is Dscout?

Dscout is a mobile research platform that connects brands with real people for in-depth consumer research. Unlike traditional surveys, Dscout uses "missions" where you record video diary entries, take photos, and share detailed feedback about products and experiences.

The platform is used by major companies like Google, Spotify, Facebook, and Nike to understand how real people interact with their products. Because the feedback is more detailed than a typical survey, the compensation is significantly higher.

Missions typically pay $25-100, with some multi-part studies paying $150 or more. The catch is that acceptance is competitive - you apply for missions and are selected based on your profile and responses.

How Dscout Works

  1. Download the app (iOS or Android)
  2. Complete your profile - detailed demographics and interests
  3. Apply for missions - answer screener questions
  4. Get selected - based on fit with research criteria
  5. Complete "entries" - video diaries, photos, written responses
  6. Get paid via PayPal after mission completion

Types of Missions

  • Express Missions: Quick $5-15 tasks, single entry
  • Diary Missions: Multi-day studies tracking experiences
  • Live Missions: Video calls with researchers
  • Product Testing: Test products and share feedback

What "Entries" Involve

Most missions require you to record short video selfies (30-90 seconds) answering questions about your experiences. You might be asked to show products you use, explain your daily routines, or share opinions while shopping.

Realistic Earnings

Tips for Getting Selected

  1. Complete your profile thoroughly: More details = better matching
  2. Apply to missions quickly: Spots fill fast
  3. Write thoughtful screener answers: Stand out from other applicants
  4. Be authentic in videos: They want real people, not performers
  5. Check the app daily: New missions post regularly
  6. Complete missions fully: Good track record helps future selection

Acceptance Rates

Expect to apply to many missions before being selected. A 10-20% acceptance rate is normal. Don't get discouraged - keep applying and your rate will improve as you complete more missions.
