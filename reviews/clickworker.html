What is Clickworker?

Clickworker is a German-based crowdsourcing platform that connects workers with micro-tasks from major companies. Founded in 2005, it's one of the oldest and most established platforms in the space, with over 4.5 million registered workers worldwide.

The main draw of Clickworker is access to UHRS (Universal Human Relevance System) - Microsoft's micro-task platform. UHRS tasks often pay better than Clickworker's native tasks and offer more consistent availability.

Tasks include data entry, web research, AI training data labeling, content creation, surveys, and more. Work availability varies significantly by region and time, which is the main frustration for users.

How Clickworker Works

  1. Create an account and complete your profile thoroughly
  2. Take assessments - these unlock different task types
  3. Access UHRS - after passing the UHRS qualification assessment
  4. Complete tasks - work appears in your dashboard when available
  5. Get paid - weekly automatic payouts once you hit the threshold

Types of Tasks

  • UHRS tasks: Search result evaluation, content moderation, data labeling - $3-12/hour
  • Text creation: Write short articles or product descriptions - $0.50-5 per piece
  • Data categorization: Label images, categorize data - $0.01-0.10 per task
  • Surveys: Market research surveys - $0.50-2 each
  • App testing: Test mobile apps and report bugs - $3-10 per test

Tips for Maximizing Earnings

  1. Get UHRS access ASAP: Complete the UHRS qualification as soon as it's available - it's where the real money is.
  2. Check frequently: Tasks appear and disappear quickly. Check multiple times per day.
  3. Maintain quality: UHRS tracks your accuracy. Low quality = ban from tasks.
  4. Complete all assessments: More qualifications = more task types available.
  5. Don't rush: Speed matters, but accuracy matters more. Bans are hard to reverse.

Potential Issues

The biggest complaint is task availability. UHRS in particular can be feast or famine - you might earn $50 one day and $0 the next because there's simply no work available.

Temporary bans from UHRS tasks are also common if your quality scores drop. These can last from hours to permanently, depending on severity.

Quality Warning

UHRS tracks your accuracy carefully. Rushing through tasks to maximize speed will get you banned. Focus on quality first, speed second.
