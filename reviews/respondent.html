What is Respondent?

Respondent is a premium research platform that connects participants with companies conducting user research studies. Unlike typical survey sites that pay cents per survey, Respondent focuses on high-value studies that can pay $50 to $200+ for a single 30-60 minute session.

Founded in 2016, Respondent has paid out over $50 million to participants and works with major companies including Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and thousands of startups. The platform primarily offers video interviews, usability tests, and in-depth research sessions rather than quick surveys.

The catch? Acceptance rates are low. Most studies require specific demographics, professional backgrounds, or user behaviors. But for those who qualify, the earning potential is unmatched in the survey/research space.

How Respondent Works

Respondent works differently from typical survey sites. Here's the process:

  1. Create a detailed profile - Fill out your professional background, demographics, and interests
  2. Browse available studies - Studies are listed with requirements, time commitment, and pay rate
  3. Apply for studies - Answer screening questions to see if you qualify
  4. Complete the session - Most are video calls via Zoom or similar platforms
  5. Get paid - Payment typically arrives within 5-10 days via PayPal

Types of Studies Available

  • Remote interviews: 30-60 minute video calls discussing products or services - typically $75-150
  • Usability testing: Test websites or apps while sharing your screen - typically $50-100
  • Focus groups: Group discussions with 4-8 participants - typically $100-200
  • Diary studies: Track behavior over days/weeks - typically $100-300 total
  • In-person studies: Visit a location for research - often $150-300+

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Highest payouts in the industry ($50-200+ per study)
  • Professional, respectful research experience
  • Reliable PayPal payments within 5-10 days
  • Studies are actually interesting and engaging
  • No spam or low-quality surveys

Cons

  • Low acceptance rates (expect 5-15% of applications to be accepted)
  • Study availability varies greatly by demographics
  • Requires video calls - not for those camera-shy
  • Must be articulate and comfortable speaking
  • Scheduling can be tricky with time zone differences

Payment Proof & Reliability

Respondent has an excellent reputation for payment reliability. The platform has paid out over $50 million to participants since 2016, and payment complaints are extremely rare.

Payments are processed via PayPal within 5-10 business days after study completion. Some researchers pay bonuses for exceptional participation.

Payment Timeline

Studies typically pay within 5-10 business days. Respondent holds funds briefly to ensure study quality before releasing payment. In our experience, payments always arrive on time.

Tips for Maximizing Earnings

  1. Complete your profile thoroughly: The more details you provide about your profession, hobbies, and demographics, the better your matching with studies.
  2. Apply quickly: Popular studies fill up fast. Check the platform daily and apply to relevant studies immediately.
  3. Be honest in screeners: Lying to qualify will get you banned. Companies verify information and Respondent takes fraud seriously.
  4. Maintain availability: Be flexible with scheduling. Studies often need specific time slots.
  5. Be professional in sessions: Good ratings from researchers lead to more invitations. Treat each session like a job interview.
  6. Use LinkedIn integration: Connecting your LinkedIn profile increases credibility and access to B2B studies.

Potential Issues

The main frustration with Respondent is the low acceptance rate. You might apply to 20 studies and only get accepted to 1-2. This is normal - don't get discouraged.

Some users report being "ghosted" after scheduling - the researcher cancels or never confirms. This is annoying but relatively rare.

Time Investment Warning

Applying to studies takes time. If you spend 30 minutes applying to studies you don't qualify for, your effective hourly rate drops. Focus on studies that closely match your profile.
