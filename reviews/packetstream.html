What is PacketStream?

PacketStream is a bandwidth sharing service that pays you to share your unused internet connection. The service routes traffic through residential IPs, which businesses use for web scraping, ad verification, and market research.

The concept is simple: install their app, let it run in the background, and earn money based on how much bandwidth is used through your connection. It's truly passive income - no surveys, no tasks, no clicking.

PacketStream pays $0.10 per GB shared, with a $5 minimum payout via PayPal. It's one of the simpler bandwidth apps with straightforward pricing.

How PacketStream Works

  1. Download the PacketStream app
  2. Install and run in the background
  3. Earn $0.10 per GB of bandwidth shared
  4. Cash out at $5 via PayPal

Technical Details

  • Resource usage: Minimal CPU/RAM impact
  • Bandwidth: Uses idle bandwidth only
  • Speed impact: Negligible on most connections
  • Data limits: Configurable maximum usage

Privacy & Security

Important Consideration

Your IP address is used by PacketStream's clients. While they filter traffic, your IP may be associated with web scraping activities. This is generally legal but review your ISP's terms of service before using.
