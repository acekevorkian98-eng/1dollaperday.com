What is UserTesting?

Imagine getting paid $10 to spend 20 minutes giving your opinion on a website or app. That's UserTesting - and yes, it's exactly as good as it sounds. The catch? You won't get unlimited tests, and you need to actually be good at giving feedback.

UserTesting is a user experience research platform that connects companies with real people who test their websites, apps, and prototypes. Major brands like Apple, Facebook, Adobe, and Microsoft use it to get genuine user feedback before launching products. As a tester, you browse their sites, complete tasks, and speak your thoughts aloud while screen recording.

Founded in 2007, UserTesting is now a publicly traded company (acquired by Thoma Bravo in 2022 for $1.3 billion). They're the industry leader in this space, which means they have the most tests available and the most established payment system. If you're articulate and can provide thoughtful feedback, this is one of the best-paying side gigs out there.

How UserTesting Works

Getting Started

  1. Sign up: Create a free account at usertesting.com/be-a-user-tester
  2. Complete your profile: Demographics, devices you own, apps you use, etc.
  3. Take a practice test: This qualifies you and teaches you the format
  4. Get rated: UserTesting reviews your practice test and rates your quality
  5. Receive test invitations: When tests match your profile, you get notified

Types of Tests

  • Standard Tests ($10): 15-20 minutes. Browse a website/app, complete tasks, speak your thoughts aloud. Most common type.
  • Live Conversations ($30-120): 30-60 minute video calls with researchers. Higher pay, requires scheduling.
  • Mobile Tests ($10): Test apps on your smartphone. Same format as standard tests.
  • Prototype Tests ($10): Test unreleased features or designs. Often interesting stuff.

The Secret: Think Aloud Protocol

UserTesting uses "think aloud" methodology - you literally narrate your thoughts as you browse. "I'm looking for the contact page... I expected it to be in the header... this menu is confusing because..." Companies pay big money for this raw, unfiltered feedback. The better you are at articulating your thought process, the higher you'll be rated and the more tests you'll get.

Payment

  • Standard tests: $10 via PayPal, 7 days after test completion
  • Live conversations: $30-120 via PayPal, 7 days after session
  • No minimum: Get paid for every completed test

Test Availability Is Limited

The main frustration with UserTesting is inconsistency. Some weeks you'll get 5+ tests; other weeks, maybe 1 or none. Tests are matched to specific demographics, and they fill up FAST - often within minutes of being posted. You can't treat this as your sole income source, but it's an excellent supplement.

What Affects Test Availability

  • Your rating: 4-5 star testers get more invitations
  • Demographics: US testers typically get more tests
  • Devices: Owning both iOS and Android doubles opportunities
  • Profile completeness: More details = more matching tests
  • Response speed: Tests fill quickly; fast responders win

How to Pass the Practice Test

Your practice test determines your rating and future test availability. Here's how to nail it:

  1. Find a quiet space: Background noise will hurt your rating. No TV, no kids, no traffic.
  2. Speak constantly: Narrate EVERYTHING. "I'm clicking here because..." "I notice that..." "This confuses me because..."
  3. Be specific: Don't say "this is nice." Say "I like how the navigation is organized by category rather than alphabetically."
  4. Point out problems: Companies WANT to hear what's wrong. Don't be polite - be honest.
  5. Complete all tasks: Even if you find them confusing, attempt everything.
  6. Check your audio: Make sure your microphone is working and audio is clear.

The Rating System

Testers are rated 1-5 stars by clients. Your rating affects how many tests you receive. Aim for 4+ stars by being thorough, articulate, and honest. Low ratings can significantly reduce your test invitations.

Tips for Getting More Tests

  1. Complete your profile thoroughly: Every detail increases matching opportunities.
  2. Enable notifications: Tests fill in minutes. Push notifications are essential.
  3. Check during business hours: Most tests post during US business hours (9 AM - 5 PM EST).
  4. Own multiple devices: iOS + Android + desktop = 3x the opportunities.
  5. Be quick on screeners: When you get a test invite, complete the screener immediately.
  6. Maintain high ratings: Quality over quantity. One bad review hurts more than skipping a test helps.
  7. Accept live conversations: They pay more and can lead to more standard test invites.
  8. Sign up for similar platforms: UserFeel, TryMyUI, and Userlytics work the same way.
